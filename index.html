Resilient Ingleside

Resilient Ingleside is a story about a collaboration between environmental nonprofits in Norfolk, VA and University of Virginia architecture students to design green infrastructure for the Ingleside neighborhood. This infrastructure will reduce flooding due to sea level rise and a poor stormwater drainage system and improve local water quality, and the collaboration itself will serve as a model for improving coastal resiliency in the region.

Credits: designing/storyboarding, filming, video/audio editing.



The James River Blues

The James River Blues is a story about scientists and resource managers research into and handling of blue catfish, an invasive species, in the James River and other rivers in the Chesapeake Bay.

Credits: filming, video/audio editing.




Aperture

Aperture is a short film about a grandmother and her granddaughter wandering through a post-nuclear-apocalyptic future. They come across a shelter-city built inside a mountain to avoid fallout, where the ghosts of the people who lived there have continued to inhabit their home in darkness.

Credits: writing/storyboarding, creative directing, modeling, animating, rendering, post-processing.

DataUSA in Virtual Reality

DataUSA VR is a data visualization and storytelling experience that uses Unreal Engine 4 and Vive. It was developed at the MIT Media Lab's Collective Learning group (formerly the Macro Connections group) under the supervision of Takahito Ito (NHK) and César Hidalgo (MIT). The experience uses data from the group's project DataUSA.

Credits: writing, designing, programming.




Transcontinental

Transcontinental is a narrative-based game about a teenager traveling across the US on a train. She finds insight and solace from conversations with strangers along the way, but also a few cold shoulders. Made with GameMaker: Studio.
